Foreword

A market finding its footing

After two years of dislocation, Rotherham is showing the early signs of a more rational commercial market — modest rental growth, sensible yields, and a quieter but more deliverable pipeline of occupier requirements.

The Bank Rate easing cycle that began in late 2025 has worked its way into commercial pricing. Prime industrial yields in the South Yorkshire sub-market have hardened back inside 6.5% and the volume of stock under offer at the end of Q3 is the strongest we have recorded since Q2 2024. Office demand remains polarised — best-in-class refurbished space at Moorgate and town-centre stock with EPC B or better is letting at our quoting tone, while older second-hand floors continue to drift on void.

South Yorkshire Mayoral Combined Authority's Growth Plan continues to direct devolution funding into Rotherham — the M1 Junction 33 / Templeborough corridor, the AMID at Manvers, and Town Centre regeneration around Forge Island all featured in occupier conversations this quarter. Public-sector lettings (DWP, NHS estate) and a steady drumbeat of regional logistics requirements (3PL, last-mile, automotive aftermarket) underpinned take-up.

This report is built from CPR's own instruction book — the lettings, sales, rent reviews and lease renewals that we and our local peer group are actively transacting. Where the data is partial we have said so. As ever, please do call us if you would like to test a specific property or strategy against the figures.

Section 02 — Office market

Polarised, but the top is moving

Rotherham's office market continues to split into two clear streams. Refurbished, EPC B-or-better space at Moorgate and the better town-centre buildings is now consistently letting at a £14.50–£16.00/sq ft tone, with shorter voids and tenant inducements normalising back to 3–6 months on a 10-year term. Older second-hand stock — particularly above ground-floor retail on Westgate and unimproved suites at the periphery — is taking longer to let and is competing on inducement rather than headline rent.

Take-up of 38,400 sq ft across 11 transactions was 12.4% ahead of Q3 2025. Professional services (accountancy, legal, recruitment, insurance broking) accounted for just under half of floorspace let, with public-sector and healthcare-adjacent occupiers contributing two larger deals.

Supply — total office availability

Total marketed availability across Rotherham fell to circa 312,000 sq ft at end Q3, the lowest reading since Q4 2022. Genuine Grade A availability is now thin — under 22,000 sq ft across all sub-markets — and is concentrated in two buildings at Moorgate.

Refurbishment is the only meaningful pipeline. Two schemes totalling roughly 18,500 sq ft are due to complete in Q1 2027 and a further 12,000 sq ft is in pre-let discussion at Forge Island Phase 2.

Demand — live requirements on CPR's book

CPR is currently holding 27 named office requirements across the borough totalling circa 64,000 sq ft. Median requirement size is 1,850 sq ft; the largest is a 6,500 sq ft consolidation move from a regional professional services firm. Roughly two-thirds of named requirements specify EPC B or better as a hard filter.

Net effective rent — after rent-free, capital contributions and dilapidations capping — sits at approximately 87% of headline tone for Grade A and 78% for secondary stock.

Sub-market spotlight — Forge Island

Forge Island has materially shifted the market's expectation for town-centre Grade A. The Phase 1 lettings completed during 2025–26 have established a £14.00–£14.50/sq ft benchmark and pulled enquiries away from older town-centre stock that would previously have been the default. We expect Phase 2 to set the next reference point in 2027 — quoting tone is likely to test £15.00/sq ft on a 10-year term.

Outlook — office

We expect prime tone to push toward £16.00/sq ft over the next four quarters as the next refurbishment cycle delivers and Grade A availability tightens further. Secondary remains the watch-out: dated, EPC C-or-worse stock continues to drift, and the gap between prime and secondary will widen. Landlords with options-to-refurbish should be running the numbers now — the rent-free / capex trade-off is currently as favourable as we have seen since 2018.

Section 03 — Industrial & logistics

Stock-constrained, demand-led

Industrial remains the strongest sector in the Rotherham economy. Take-up of 187,500 sq ft across 14 transactions was 9.6% behind a strong Q3 2025 — but this reflects supply scarcity rather than weakening demand. CPR's named requirements register is at a five-year high and we are closing deals faster than units are coming back to the market.

The M1 / M18 corridor, anchored by Junctions 32, 33 and 34, continues to draw 3PL, automotive aftermarket and last-mile occupiers. The Advanced Manufacturing Innovation District (AMID) at Manvers is feeding a steady drumbeat of design-and-build enquiries from Tier-1 and Tier-2 manufacturers — three of those moved into solicitor-led negotiations in Q3.

Vacancy & supply

Industrial vacancy across the Rotherham travel-to-work area is at 3.4% at end Q3, modestly tighter than Q2 (3.7%) and well below the long-run average of 6.1%. Sub-25,000 sq ft availability is the tightest band, with under 60,000 sq ft on the market across 11 units.

Planning & pipeline

Three industrial consents progressed through Rotherham MBC planning in Q3: (i) c.95,000 sq ft speculative scheme at Templeborough; (ii) Phase 3 of AMID at Manvers (c.140,000 sq ft over three plots); (iii) reserved-matters approval for a 28,000 sq ft trade scheme at Eastwood. Earliest deliveries from this cohort are Q3 2027.

Sub-market spotlight — Manvers AMID

The Advanced Manufacturing Innovation District is now the single biggest source of design-and-build enquiries in the South Yorkshire industrial market outside the Sheffield AMP. Three pre-let conversations advanced in Q3 across precision engineering, advanced metals and a battery-systems Tier-2. Quoting tone on D&B is testing £9.50/sq ft on 15-year terms — a level that would have looked aspirational two years ago.

Section 04 — Retail market

Town centre re-anchoring; out-of-town steady

Town-centre retail remains in transition. Forge Island's leisure and F&B-led offer is reshaping the gravity of the centre, with positive footfall reads on College Street and around All Saints Square. Westgate continues to see vacancy churn — but encouragingly, three of the four units re-let in Q3 went to independent operators (two F&B, one specialist retail) at headline rents around £18,500–£24,000 per annum.

Out-of-town retail at Parkgate Retail Park and Cortonwood is the strongest performing retail format in the borough. Both schemes are reporting low single-digit vacancy and a steady pipeline of mid-market requirements (value fashion, pet, homeware, branded F&B drive-thru).

Section 05 — Investment market

Buyers are back — selectively

The combination of a more settled rate environment and modest rental growth has brought a wider buyer pool back into the Rotherham investment market in Q3. Industrial remains the deepest market by some margin, with prime stock attracting competitive bidding from regional propcos and a small number of private investors. Office investment is more selective: well-let, EPC B-or-better stock is tradeable; older multi-let secondary continues to be priced as a refurbishment / asset-management play.

Section 06 — Forward view

Q4 2026 and into 2027

Our base case is for a more confident occupier market into Q4, supported by the rate cycle, devolution funding flowing through the SYMCA growth plan, and a continued shortage of best-in-class stock.

Q4 2026 — what we expect

Office. Take-up to finish 2026 at or marginally ahead of the five-year average. Prime tone holds at £15.50/sq ft, with the next refurbishment cycle setting up a push toward £16.00 in early 2027.

Industrial. Take-up volumes stock-constrained rather than demand-constrained. Prime rents to test £9.50/sq ft on the next D&B settlement at Manvers or Templeborough.

Retail. Town centre to continue re-anchoring around Forge Island and All Saints Square — independents are doing the heavy lifting. Out-of-town remains the strongest format.

Investment. Industrial prime yields to harden a further 10–15 bps if the rate cycle continues as expected. Office investment a bottom-up, building-by-building market.

2027 themes to watch

  • 01.  EPC B by 2030 — landlord capex decisions on secondary stock can no longer be deferred. The cost-to-comply gap is the dominant variable in valuation conversations.
  • 02.  Forge Island Phase 2 — sets the next town-centre Grade A reference point and will pull more enquiry into the centre.
  • 03.  AMID Manvers — Phase 3 consents bring c.140,000 sq ft of D&B-able plots into the conversation. Tier-1 / Tier-2 manufacturing demand persists.
  • 04.  SYMCA devolution funding — visible in the M1 J33 corridor and Town Centre regeneration. Public-sector occupier demand likely to remain a feature.
  • 05.  Refurbishment economics — rent-free / capex trade-off currently as favourable as we have seen in seven years. Landlords with optionality should be running numbers now.

Risks to the base case

(i) A reversal in the rate cycle would pause the yield-hardening dynamic. (ii) An EPC compliance acceleration would bring refurbishment costs forward, biting into landlord cashflow before rental uplift catches up. (iii) Construction cost inflation continues to be the key sensitivity on D&B economics.

Section 08 — Methodology & disclaimers

How this report is built

Illustrative draft — figures in this document are demonstration data

Data sources

This report is compiled from four sources, in priority order: (i) CPR's own instruction book — lettings, sales, rent reviews, lease renewals and valuations transacted or reviewed by the practice in the reference quarter; (ii) confirmed peer-group transactions, cross-checked with the relevant agent or solicitor; (iii) Rotherham MBC planning records and the South Yorkshire MCA growth plan publications; (iv) HM Land Registry records for completed investment transactions.

Where a figure is drawn from a single confirmed source, or where the data set is partial, the relevant section narrative says so. Confidential transactions are reported in aggregate only.

Definitions

Prime rent / yield. The headline rent or yield achievable on a notional best-in-class building of the relevant type, let on standard institutional terms (typically 10 years, FRI, with five-year reviews) to a covenant of investment grade.

Take-up. Total floorspace let or sold to an occupier in the reference period, measured net internal area for offices and gross internal area for industrial.

Net effective rent. Headline rent adjusted for rent-free, capital contributions and any other tenant inducement, expressed over the term certain.

Methodology notes

Sub-market boundaries follow CPR's working geography for Rotherham, which broadly aligns with Rotherham MBC ward groupings. The "hinterland" coverage extends to Wath-upon-Dearne, Maltby, Dinnington, Manvers and the Sheffield-Rotherham boundary along Templeborough.

YoY comparisons are quarter-on-quarter same-period — Q3 2026 vs Q3 2025. QoQ comparisons (used for yields) are sequential quarters — Q3 2026 vs Q2 2026. Five-year averages cover Q3 2021 to Q3 2025 inclusive.

Yields are reported on a Net Initial Yield basis on let stock and on a notional reversionary basis where stock is vacant or under-rented.
