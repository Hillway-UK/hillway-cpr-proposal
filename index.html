Introduction

Following our meeting at your office on Wednesday 29 April with you, Lisa and Nikki, this proposal sets out how Hillway will embed Claude-based AI into Commercial Property Rotherham, automate the workflows that consume the most time in your week, and leave you with a tech stack you and your team can run yourselves.

The aim is straightforward: free Lisa’s workload before she leaves, give Nikki a system she can rely on, and give you the capacity to take on more instructions without taking on more cost.

02

What We Understand

Six themes emerged from the meeting where AI will deliver immediate, measurable time savings.

Lisa’s Departure (Operational Risk)

Lisa has handed in her notice. With the right setup, much of the manual work she carries today can be automated before she leaves — but the window is tight. Capturing her processes while she is still on the team is the priority of the first six weeks.

Invoicing

Lisa described the current process — a Word template edited each month or quarter, exported to PDF, attached to an email, sent client by client. Across hundreds of invoices a cycle, this is where most of her time goes. A connected Xero + Claude flow can produce, check and dispatch the same invoices in minutes rather than days.

Cash-In Reconciliation

Money arrives, then has to be recorded against the transaction record, the rent statement, and a separate Excel sheet per client. Lisa rechecks every figure repeatedly because the process is manual and error-prone. Bank-feed-driven reconciliation through Xero, with branded client statements produced automatically, removes most of that double-handling.

Compliance Tracking

EICR, gas, EPC and other compliance certificates currently sit in emails and folders. Nikki is working through them manually, ticking dates on a list. Claude can read every certificate in your inbox and drive, extract the expiry dates, populate a dashboard, and add reminders to the calendar at three-month, one-month and one-week intervals.

Lease, Valuation & Marketing Reports

Lease abstracts, valuation reports, and marketing brochures are still produced largely by hand. With your data structured the right way, Claude can pull lease terms, comparables, VOA, EPC and planning data, and produce branded first drafts. You and Neil keep the professional judgement; the assembly work disappears.

Rightmove & Brochure Workflow

PowerPoint brochures and Rightmove uploads are currently a manual back-end task. Until a Rightmove MCP exists, browser automation can populate the listing back-end from your data; brochure production can be templated and produced in seconds. Your Burgess Commercial legacy agent ID is still showing on Rightmove — worth resolving as part of the work.

03

Scope of Work

A three-month embedded engagement, structured in three phases, with one consultant day per week on site or remote as suits.

Phase 1

Foundation & Discovery (Weeks 1–2)

  • Install Claude Code on Neil’s and Nikki’s machines (and Lisa’s for the handover period); set up mobile access
  • Connect MCP servers to Xero, Microsoft 365 (email, OneDrive, calendar, Outlook contacts), Rightmove back-end (browser automation initially), VOA, EPC register, and Land Registry
  • Build the “CPR Wiki” — a structured central context store covering your clients, properties, suppliers, standard processes and templates, so Claude has the same background a member of staff would
  • Sit with Lisa to capture every step of her invoicing and cash-in processes — the priority is locking down her tacit knowledge before she leaves
  • Issue a workflow inventory with prioritised automation roadmap for sign-off before build starts
Phase 2

Priority Workflow Build (Weeks 3–10)

  • Invoicing engine — rent and service charge invoicing automated end-to-end: Claude generates the invoices in Xero, drafts the covering emails, attaches the PDFs, and queues them for one-click approval. Designed and tested alongside Lisa while she is still in post.
  • Cash-in reconciliation — bank feed into Xero, automated allocation to invoices, branded rent statements produced for client landlords, exception list flagged daily
  • Compliance tracker — expiry dates extracted from certificates, central dashboard, calendar reminders, automated chasing emails for renewals
  • Lease report engine — PDF lease in, structured abstract out: rent, term, breaks, repair, alienation, user, service charge, with source citations to the lease itself for verification
  • Meeting capture — meeting transcripts feed back into the wiki automatically; action points become follow-up reminders; client conversations stay searchable
Phase 3

Training, Handover & Phase 2 Roadmap (Weeks 11–13)

  • Hands-on training for Neil and Nikki: prompt patterns, voice dictation, mobile use, common workflows, when to override
  • Written SOPs for every automation built, so the system survives future staff changes
  • Phase 2 roadmap covering valuation reports, brochure and Rightmove automation, utility recharge, and any further workflows surfaced during the engagement
  • Final review with Neil to confirm what is live, what is outstanding, and whether to continue under the Evolve retainer
Optional

Evolve — Ongoing AI Tech Partner (Month 4 onwards)

  • Up to two consultant days per month plus async Slack/email support for Neil and Nikki
  • New automations built as workflows surface; the tech stack evolves with the business
  • Continuous integration of new Claude features, new MCPs (e.g. Rightmove and CoStar when their APIs land), new property tools
  • Quarterly review: what is working, where to next, what to decommission
  • No minimum term; 30 days’ notice on either side

Recommended Sequencing

Lisa has confirmed she can extend her notice to the end of June. We strongly recommend doing so. It gives us six weeks of overlap in which her invoicing and cash-in processes are captured, automated, and tested with her in the room. Anything less and those workflows transfer cold to Nikki at the worst possible moment.
