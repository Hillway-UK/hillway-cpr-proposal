02

Six themes from the meeting where AI delivers measurable time savings from week three onwards.

Operational risk

Lisa’s departure.

Lisa has handed in her notice. With the right setup most of her manual work is automated before she leaves — but the window is tight. Capturing her processes while she is still on the team is the priority of the first six weeks.

Phase 1 — Priority

Invoicing.

A Word template edited each cycle, exported to PDF, attached to an email, sent client by client. Across hundreds of invoices a quarter, this is where most of Lisa’s time goes. A connected Xero + Claude flow produces, checks and dispatches the same invoices in minutes.

Phase 1 — Priority

Cash-in reconciliation.

Money arrives, then has to be recorded against the transaction record, the rent statement, and a separate Excel sheet per client. Bank-feed reconciliation through Xero, with branded landlord statements produced automatically, removes most of the double-handling.

Phase 1 — Priority

Compliance tracking.

EICR, gas, EPC and other certificates sit in emails and folders. Claude reads every certificate, extracts the expiry, populates a dashboard and adds calendar reminders at 90, 30 and 7 days — plus chases suppliers for renewal.

Phase 2 — Candidate

Lease, valuation & marketing reports.

Lease abstracts, valuation reports, marketing brochures — still produced largely by hand. With your data structured properly, Claude pulls lease terms, comparables, VOA, EPC and planning data, and produces branded first drafts. You keep the professional judgement; the assembly disappears.

Phase 2 — Candidate

Rightmove & brochure workflow.

PowerPoint brochures and Rightmove uploads are a manual back-end task. Browser automation populates listings from your data; brochures are templated. Worth fixing the Burgess Commercial legacy agent ID still showing on Rightmove as part of this.
